How to Choose the Right Pool Builder

Choosing a pool builder is one of the more consequential decisions in a home improvement project. The wrong choice costs time, money, and a lot of frustration. Here's what to actually look for — regardless of who you hire.

We share this because informed clients make better decisions — and because we're confident Paragon holds up under every one of these questions.

Talk to Paragon
  • Verify licensing, bonding, and insurance.

    Every legitimate pool contractor in Utah should carry a valid contractor's license and liability insurance. Ask for documentation before any conversation about design or pricing.

  • Ask who is managing the build — day to day.

    Many companies sell the project with their best person, then hand it to someone else. Know who your actual point of contact will be from start to finish, and what their authority is.

  • Talk to references from similar-scale projects.

    A contractor who built $80K pools may not be ready for a $300K backyard environment. Ask for references from projects comparable in scope to yours.

  • Understand their subcontractor relationships.

    Most pool builders use subcontractors. That's normal. What matters is how they vet them, how they manage them, and who is accountable when something isn't right.

  • Get a detailed written scope of work — not just a number.

    A proposal that says "pool and spa — $175,000" tells you almost nothing. You need itemized materials, specifications, and timeline commitments in writing before you sign anything.

  • Be skeptical of the lowest bid.

    In custom construction, aggressive pricing usually means one of three things: lower-grade materials, less experienced labor, or a scope that will expand after you've signed. Quality has a cost. Understand what you're actually comparing.

  • Ask about their design and approval process.

    How do they take your ideas from concept to final design? Do you get 3D renderings? How many revisions are included? Is there a dedicated design phase before construction begins?

  • Trust your gut about the person — not just the company.

    After you've checked every box, ask yourself a harder question: do I actually trust this person? A pool project runs for months. Things will go sideways at some point — they always do. The real question isn't whether your builder is competent. It's how they're going to act when something goes wrong. Will they own it or deflect it? Protect you or protect themselves? You're going to be in the trenches with this person. Make sure it's someone worth being in the trenches with.
